Study Abroad Packing

Posted by Chloe Andersen on April 22, 2024
Important Documents & Packing List

IMPORTANT DOCUMENTS & MONEY

  • Money
    • Cash ($50 in small bills)
    • Credit/debit cards - more than one
    • Airline ticket & ITINERARY from BYU travel (keep in carry-on luggage)
    • Passport (signed)
    • Frequent flier numbers, if applicable
    • Copies of your passport and credit cards - front and back, place in your luggage or carry-on (separate from originals)
    • A few small U.S. bills ($1 and $5) for shopping. (Group tips are handled by the program)
    • List of prescriptions
    • Driver’s License or other, second government-issued ID
    • Insurance Cards (BYU GeoBlue and personal)
    • Hard or soft copies of company country briefings & CulltureGrams
  • PACKING FOR THE TRIP
    • One suitcase (no bigger than 30”) and one carry-on (no bigger than 19”, preferably only a personal item). Make sure all have ID tags. Minimize a personal item (may need to be combined with the carry-on or the carry-on may need to be checked).
    • Around the belly or neck pouch and small daypack.

CLOTHING

  • 3-4 blouses/shirts (comfortable and conservative) for travel
  • 1-2 business casual blouses/shirts
  • 1-2 travel casual pants/knee-length shorts/skirts
  • 1-2 pair dressy or business casual slacks for dinners, etc.
  • Business visit clothes - for women -- Skirt and blouse or dress. Might overlap with Sunday outfit.
  • Sunday clothes - Women- Skirt and blouse or dress
  • Shoes & Socks:
    • Sunday/business visit shoes
    • Sturdy, comfortable walking shoes, already broken in
    • Flip flops for use at beach/hotel
  • Accessories
    • Belts
    • Earrings
    • Necklaces
    • Watch
    • Hair accessories
  • Underwear - enough for 2 weeks or longer
    • Panties
    • Bras
    • Simple sleepwear
  • Sun gear
    • Swimming suit
    • Cover up / Sun Dress
    • Sunglasses
    • Hat
    • Sunscreen?
  • Exercise clothing (conservative), if desired
  • Sweatshirt / Jacket / Sweater (bring on plane)
  • Rain jacket, a poly-type with hood. Waterproof, breathable fabric. Optional: small umbrella (also good for shade in hot climates)

TOILETRIES

  • Medical supplies (in your carry on) for colds, headaches, muscle aches, etc. band aids, stomach troubles
  • Toothbrush / Paste / Flossers
  • Comb / Brush / Hair styling items?
  • Deodorant - small sample size is best
  • Nail clippers (NOT in carry on)
  • Sunscreen, insect repellant
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Shaving - sample size cream - razor NOT in carry on
  • Several handkerchiefs (wipe sweat or hand drying in some restrooms)
  • Hair products / lotion
  • Feminine hygiene
  • Small Kleenex packet for toilet paper

ELECTRONICS

  • Power converter AND plug adapters - bring spares
  • Extra batteries / chargers / Power banks for electronics (NO LITHIUM BATTERIES IN CHECKED LUGGAGE!!!)
  • Laptop (optional)
  • Phone / Camera
  • Earbuds

OTHER

  • Writing pad / Pens - some kind of compact note-taking system
  • Erasable pens
  • Water bottle
  • Reading material for planes / buses
  • Optional for laundry - dirty clothes bag, small travel size sample powder detergent packets
  • Small personal travel pillow for bus / plane + eye shades if needed

TO DO BEFORE YOU GO!!!

  • Contact credit / bank card companies, let them know which countries you will be visiting. Ask about fees and currency conversions, etc and consider tradeoffs between foreign transaction fees and exchange rate differences.
  • Check with your own insurance carrier as to what is covered abroad ans what is not (we all have GeoBlue insurance too)
  • Check with your cell phone service provider to put on an international service package or set up Internet- based contact mechanism (Google Chat, etc) account
  • Immunizations seem to always be “recommended by the CDC for travel abroad (if immunizations are required, your director will inform you)
